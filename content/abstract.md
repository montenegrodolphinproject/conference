# Instructions on Submitting Abstracts

**Deadline for submission of abstracts: 1 November 2017 (23:00 GMT)**<a name="deadline"></a>


Only one abstract per author can be submitted, in one of three different formats: long-talk, short-talk and scientific poster. Long-talks must be presented by the first author, while short talks and scientific posters can be presented by the co-authors.

Upon submitting you will be asked to attach a document containing the following:

**Abstract title**: no more than 20 words.
**Authors names**: authors names, surnames and institutes/affiliations.
**Abstract**: one continuous paragraph with a word limit of 250. The abstract should be uploaded in MS word format. Please note PDF will not be accepted.

All the submitted abstracts will be reviewed and evaluated by 2 anonymous reviewers and will be selected based on merit. Once a submission is accepted, the author must confirm the registration by sending an email to info@dmad.org.tr

**Deadline for registration: 1 December 2017 (23:00 GMT)**

The presenters of long-talks and short-talks that have been accepted must provide their presentation slides by the 20th of January, 2018 to the email: info@dmad.org.tr

The presenters of scientific posters must send the PDF version of their posters by the 20th of January, 2018 to the email: info@dmad.org.tr

## Information about presentation types
### Long talks
Long-talks must be presented by the first author and can last a maximum of 20 minutes, followed by 5 minutes for questions. The presenter will be notified when there are 5 minutes left.

### Short talks
Short-talks can be presented by the first authors as well as co-authors and can last a maximum of 5 minutes, followed by 2 minutes for questions. The presenter will be notified when there is 1 minute left. Short-talks presentation must be no longer than 10 slides.

### Scientific Posters
**Size**: maximum size 85 cm (width) x 120 cm (height) - A0 format
**Author picture**: the poster must include a photo of the presenting author (with surname) at the top left corner
**Title**: not less than 96 pt
**Text**: not less than 30 pt
**Conclusions**: not less than 72 pt
**Graphs and figures**: the poster should include graphs and figures as these are the most effective ways of transferring information, more effective  than tables or text.

## Please note:
- avoid using copyrighted images unless you are the copyright holder or have the permission to use them;
- bring black and white copies (A4 format) of your poster with you, to distribute them to the conference participants.
